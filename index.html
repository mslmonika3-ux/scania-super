Nomination: Team of the Year · Client: Scania Commercial Vehicles

Steady PR. Stronger trust. 10/10 Radar.

Scania wanted fewer moving parts and more certainty. The brief wasn’t “more coverage”. It was a steady team.

Core job: Retain the client, rebuild confidence.
Pain Point: Discontinuity + attrition fatigue.
Approach: Run the machine. Deliver the proof. Be the counsel.
The client-servicing win Ownership. Rhythm. Counsel. A newly ramped team delivered consistently across routine work and critical moments.

The case, in one line

We rebuilt Scania’s confidence by designing a system that holds in three conditions: the everyday, the spotlight, and the storm.

1
Stabilise delivery (And do it fast) Locked ownership, rhythms, and a “no surprises” operating model to rebuild confidence.
2
Rebuild trust through outcomes Proved reliability through a major launch and consistently crisp execution.
3
Lead in moments that matter Owned reputation response during the Kurnool bus tragedy—controlled, coordinated, and on-message.
4
Own the full moment Became the partner for end-to-end event counsel: Event flow, speaker recommendations, and stakeholder coordination.

The challenge

The risk was in the operating layer, not the output.

Obstacle 1

Continuity was broken

Frequent changes and handovers had eroded confidence and slowed momentum.

Obstacle 2

Attrition had become the headline

The relationship kept resetting.The client desired stability in the team.

Obstacle 3

Trust deficit, not output deficit

We needed to prove steadiness under pressure—especially with a relatively new execution team.

The PR plan

A continuity-led model that turned delivery into 10/10 trust.

Operating model

Business continuity, engineered

  • Single-threaded ownership and clear escalation paths
  • Set engagement rhythms with clear actionables
  • Early risk radar, ensuring zero last-minute surprises for the client
Integration

Relationship reset with substance

  • Always-on responsiveness with structured updates
  • Sharper stakeholder management across internal and external teams
  • Consistency of voice and judgement, across channels
Leadership

Earned ‘first-call’ status

  • Strategic counsel beyond press—extending to events, KOLs, and marketing
  • Executive-ready talk tracks and messaging alignment
  • Hands-on counsel for all high-visibility moments
Client-first

Prove it in high-stakes moments

  • Launch execution with tight narrative control
  • Reputation/crisis response: Kurnool bus tragedy
  • Customer event support: Guest recommendations + event-flow coordination
